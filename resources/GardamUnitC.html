Non-trivial units of complex group rings

Abstract.

The Kaplansky unit conjecture for group rings is false in characteristic zero.

Group rings, unit conjecture

Introduction

Let G𝐺G be a torsion-free group and K𝐾K be a field. The question of whether the group ring K​[G]𝐾delimited-[]𝐺K[G] can have any units other than the trivial units, i.e. the non-zero scalar multiples of group elements, dates back to Higman’s thesis [Hig40, p. 77] and is generally known as the Kaplansky unit conjecture. An important consequence of a given K​[G]𝐾delimited-[]𝐺K[G] satisfying the conjecture is that it has no zero divisors [Pas85, Lemma 13.1.2].

Once a counterexample to the unit conjecture was finally given in characteristic 222 [Gar21] and then generalized to arbitrary positive characteristic [Mur21], the obvious question was whether this phenomenon is simply an accident of positive characteristic that cannot be found in characteristic 00. The characteristic 00 case is the most interesting: the topological motivation such as underlies Higman’s thesis is focussed on the integral group ring ℤ​[G]ℤdelimited-[]𝐺\mathbb{Z}[G] and in an analytic setting such as operator algebras one generally restricts attention to K=ℂ𝐾ℂK=\mathbb{C}. For instance, one way to give a counterexample to the Atiyah conjecture on integrality of L2superscript𝐿2L^{2}-Betti numbers would be to find G𝐺G such that ℂ​[G]ℂdelimited-[]𝐺\mathbb{C}[G] has zero divisors, but this necessitates ℂ​[G]ℂdelimited-[]𝐺\mathbb{C}[G] having non-trivial units. Moreover, a counterexample in characteristic 00 necessarily gives a counterexample in characteristic p𝑝p for all but finitely many p𝑝p.

Finding the solution

The problem of finding a non-trivial unit in ℂ​[P]ℂdelimited-[]𝑃\mathbb{C}[P] resisted many attempts of the author over the last three years and surely attracted the attention of many others; this problem of course looks easier in hindsight.

A very natural idea is to attempt to lift the solution over ℤ/2​ℤℤ2ℤ\mathbb{Z}/2\mathbb{Z} to ℤ/2n​ℤℤsuperscript2𝑛ℤ\mathbb{Z}/2^{n}\mathbb{Z} for increasing n𝑛n so as to arrive at a solution over the ring of 222-adic integers. The obstacle here is that ℤ2subscriptℤ2\mathbb{Z}_{2} has no square root of −11-1.

Finding a unit such that it and its inverse are supported on the corresponding 212121-element sets means solving a large systems of quadratic equations in 424242 variables. One should assume the units are normalized (have coefficients summing to 111); this means in particular that the trivial units are a 00-dimensional set. After Bartholdi’s coherent reformulation of [Gar21, Lemma 1] in terms of automorphisms of the group ring [Bar23], one could attempt to solve the system of quadratic equations over ℂℂ\mathbb{C} by adding these additional constraints. As P𝑃P has abelianization ℤ/4⊕ℤ/4direct-sumℤ4ℤ4\mathbb{Z}/4\oplus\mathbb{Z}/4 we have the freedom to consider characters taking values other than ±1plus-or-minus1\pm 1. That reduces the number of variables from 424242 to 111111 (ϕ0subscriptitalic-ϕ0\phi_{0} has precisely one fixpoint in supp⁡(γ)supp𝛾\operatorname{supp}(\gamma)). It is more efficient to enumerate over the 44superscript444^{4} choices of a pair of characters χ0,χ1subscript𝜒0subscript𝜒1\chi_{0},\chi_{1} than to express them using additional variables. The resulting collection of systems of equations can be solved in a matter of seconds for example using singular [DGPS22] via sage [SD21], even when performing the Gröbner basis computation directly over ℚℚ\mathbb{Q} instead of over 𝔽psubscript𝔽𝑝\mathbb{F}_{p} for some large prime p𝑝p.

The automorphisms θ0subscript𝜃0\theta_{0}, θ1subscript𝜃1\theta_{1} of ℂ​[P]ℂdelimited-[]𝑃\mathbb{C}[P] are arguably unnatural, as one does not get the desirable property of pairs of elements α𝛼\alpha, α′superscript𝛼′\alpha^{\prime} satisfying the symmetry as described by [Gar21, Lemma 1] that α​α′𝛼superscript𝛼′\alpha\alpha^{\prime} automatically vanishes outside an index 222 subgroup of P𝑃P. Nonetheless, such a trick can only work for virtually abelian groups, whereas one naturally wishes to understand the units of other torsion-free groups. The author knows one other torsion-free group supporting non-trivial units over 𝔽2subscript𝔽2\mathbb{F}_{2}, and here one again has symmetry but in an unexpected way, emphasizing the point that [Gar21, Lemma 1] is not the end of the story of symmetry for units. This is presented in the following section.

However, it turns out that one can solve the problem without imposing these symmetry constraints using the state of the art software msolve [BES21]. The time needed to compute a Gröbner basis is only on the scale of hours but one needs a machine with copious memory. This Gröbner basis itself has limited value, as the computation is performed modulo a large prime p𝑝p, the variety it defines has dimension 00, and the basis polynomials are complicated (with coefficients that are seemingly random elements of 𝔽psubscript𝔽𝑝\mathbb{F}_{p}). As the system has 171717 isolated trivial solutions, this is perhaps not surprising. We can however avoid the issue of the trivial solutions by localizing a pair of the variables (enumerating over different cases so as to be exhaustive). After doing this, the computed Gröbner basis being non-trivial already tells us that there is a non-trivial solution (at least modulo p𝑝p) but even better: the coefficients are ±1∈𝔽pplus-or-minus1subscript𝔽𝑝\pm 1\in\mathbb{F}_{p} so that it is clear how to construct a Gröbner basis over ℚℚ\mathbb{Q} and thus extract solutions over ℂℂ\mathbb{C}.
