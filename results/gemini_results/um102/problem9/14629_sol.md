## Theorem: For the linear differential equation \( \dot{x}=y \) and \( \dot{y}=-x-y). Prove that the origin is circulation.


## Proof: (d) $x = y$
$\frac{dn}{dy} = y$

$y = -x - y$
$\frac{dy}{dn} = -n - y$.

$dn = y \, dy$.
$\int dn = \int y \, dy$

$dy = -n \, dn - y \, dn$.
$\int dy = - \int n \, dn - \int y \, dn$.

$n = \frac{y^2}{2}$.

$y = -\frac{n^2}{2} - yn$.

$2n = y^2$.

$2y = -n^2 - 2ny$.

$y^2 - 2n = 0$.

$n^2 + 2ny + 2y = 0$.

$y^2 - 2n = n^2 + 2ny + 2y$.

$n^2 - y^2 + 2ny + 2n + 2y = 0 = f$.

$\Delta f = 2n - 2y + 2y + 2 + 2 = 2n + 4$

At origin $\Delta f = 4$.

At origin it is a single.
